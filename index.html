Conclusion
To conclude throughout the investigation process, I have discovered that the easing and un-easing of restrictions during the Covid-19 pandemic played a role in the rise of reported cases. I have discovered that this became evident during the visualization of my investigation. During the months of September â€“ October displayed a large spike in Covid-19 cases, in fact the spike in October was the hardest spike, with re-introduction of restriction in December resulting an increase Covid-19 cases. This may be partly due to the extended period of easier restrictions and the restrictions being brought back during the month of Christmas, and people wanting to spend time with their loved ones, family, and friends.

The visualizations that I have used throughout my investigation is quite unique as it tells the story of what being told straight away without much background understanding of that dataset, other than reading the title. The visualization that I have used throughout my investigation has been seen before. However, I found the direct direction that each chart displays tells the story of each data point. Showing any pattern and/or trend especially with the advanced charts (Waterfall chart and Pareto chart).

A pattern that I discovered during investigation that I could not have seen before when viewing the raw data is the spike in young healthcare workers receiving Covid-19. I had thought that the younger generation of healthcare workers would be affected with the easing and un-easing of restrictions. However, the age group and how young they were was a shock to me.

Future recommended work
My recommendations and directions for further analysis on this research would be to look deeper into way the number of Covid-19 cases spiked during the restrictive periods. I would recommend a day-to-day view on the number cases during those spikes alongside an infection source, to attempt to discover if the restrictions placed were 1 of 2 things.

  1. Helping the publics motivation by reducing the spread of Covid-19
  2. Creating a sense of unease that results in public refusal to obey the restrictions resulted from the frequent easing and un-easing.
